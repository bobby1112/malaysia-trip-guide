Penang Food Paradise

Hokkien Mee

A rich, spicy prawn noodle soup (different from the dark soy-sauce version in Kuala Lumpur) Served with yellow noodles and rice vermicelli, topped with slices of pork, prawns, bean sprouts, boiled egg, and crispy fried shallots.

Penang Asam Laksa

Penang Laksa, also known as Asam Laksa, is a tangy, spicy, and fish-based noodle soup â€” a must-try for anyone visiting the island. Served with thick rice noodles, shredded fish, cucumber, pineapple, mint leaves, onion, torch ginger flower, and a drizzle of thick prawn paste (hei ko) on top.

Apam Balik

Apam Balik is a beloved local street snack or dessert, often found at night markets and roadside stalls. It's a crispy-edged pancake made from a batter of flour, eggs, sugar, and coconut milk, folded over a filling of crushed peanuts, sweet corn, and sugar.
