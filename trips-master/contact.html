Frequently Asked Questions

“Do I need a visa to visit Malaysia?”

— It depends on your nationality. Citizens from over 60 countries (including the USA, UK, Canada, Australia, and most EU countries) can enter visa-free for up to 90 days. However, travelers from certain countries must apply for a visa before arrival. You can check the official Malaysian Immigration Department website for the latest visa requirements.

“What is the best way to travel around Malaysia?”

— The best way to travel around Malaysia depends on where you're going. In cities like Kuala Lumpur, public transport such as LRT, Monorail, and buses are convenient. For longer distances, trains like KTM and ETS are popular. Car services like Grab is ideal for more flexibility, especially in rural areas. For island destinations, domestic flights or ferries are often the quickest options.

“Is Malaysia expensive to travel?”

— Malaysia is generally considered affordable for travelers. Accommodation, food, and public transportation are reasonably priced. Street food is delicious and cheap, while mid-range hotels and budget guesthouses are widely available. However, costs can rise in tourist-heavy areas or luxury resorts. Overall, Malaysia provides good value for money

“Do I need vaccines before visiting Malaysia?”

— Before visiting Malaysia, it's recommended to be up to date on routine vaccines like tetanus, polio and etc. Moreover, vaccines for hepatitis A, hepatitis B, and typhoid are often advised. Malaria prevention might also be necessary if traveling to certain regions. It's a good idea to consult with a healthcare provider or travel clinic before your trip.
