About Sabah

Sabah, fondly known as the “Land Below the Wind”, is a captivating state located in the northern region of Borneo, Malaysia’s largest island. Framed by the sparkling waters of the South China Sea and the Sulu Sea, Sabah is a land of stunning contrasts — where majestic mountains, ancient rainforests, and vibrant coral reefs coexist in harmony.

At the heart of its natural wonders stands the iconic Mount Kinabalu, Southeast Asia’s highest peak and a UNESCO World Heritage Site that draws climbers and nature lovers from around the globe. Offshore, islands like Sipadan, Mabul, and Mataking boast some of the world’s most incredible diving spots, teeming with marine life and vibrant corals.

Sabah is not just a haven for outdoor adventurers — it is also a cultural mosaic, home to over 30 indigenous groups including the Kadazan-Dusun, Bajau, Murut, and Rungus. Each community brings unique traditions, languages, and festivals such as the Kaamatan Harvest Festival, where music, dance, and ancestral rituals come alive.

From the Sepilok Orangutan Rehabilitation Centre to the serene Kinabatangan River,Sabah’s rich biodiversity offers countless opportunities to connect with wildlife, including pygmy elephants, sun bears, and proboscis monkeys.The bustling capital city of Kota Kinabalu blends modernity with heritage, offering lively markets, delicious Sabahan cuisine, and warm hospitality.

Whether you seek adventure, tranquility, cultural immersion, or natural beauty, Sabah welcomes you with open arms and endless discoveries.

Nature & Adventure

Sabah is a heaven for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. From the towering Mount Kinabalu, Southeast Asia’s highest peak, to the breathtaking rainforests and pristine beaches, Sabah offers unparalleled experiences.

  • Mount Kinabalu: At 4,095 meters, Mount Kinabalu is the highest peak in Southeast Asia. Known for its dramatic landscapes and challenging terrain, it is a must-visit destination for any hiking enthusiast.

  • Coral Reefs & Diving: Sabah is home to some of the world’s most incredible dive spots like Sipadan Island,where divers experience vibrant coral reefs and swim alongside diverse marine life.

  • Cave Adventure: Sabah is home to some of the world’s most spectacular and diverse cave systems, offering thrilling opportunities for spelunking and exploration.

Culture & Heritage

Sabah is a melting pot of cultures, home to over 30 indigenous groups. Discover the rich traditions, languages, and customs that have shaped this beautiful region.

  • Indigenous Tribes: Experience the diverse indigenous cultures of Sabah, including the Kadazan-Dusun, Bajau, Murut, and Rungus. Visit the Mari Mari Cultural Village to witness traditional dances, music, and crafts.

  • Kaamatan Harvest Festival: The Kaamatan Festival, also known as the Harvest Festival, is an annual celebration in May where Sabahan tribes come together to celebrate their agricultural heritage with music, dance, and rice wine.

  • Traditional Handicrafts: Discover beautiful handmade crafts like woven baskets, beadwork, and traditional clothing that reflect the heritage and creativity of Sabah’s indigenous communities.

Wildlife & Conservation

Sabah is a biodiversity hotspot, home to some of the world’s most endangered species. Explore Sabah’s lush rainforests, river systems, and conservation centers to witness wildlife in their natural habitat.

  • Sepilok Orangutan Rehabilitation Centre: Visit the Sepilok Orangutan Rehabilitation Centre to learn about the conservation efforts aimed at saving the endangered orangutans. Watch them being rehabilitated and reintroduced to the wild.

  • Kinabatangan River Wildlife Safari: Take a boat tour along the Kinabatangan River to observe wildlife such as pygmy elephants, proboscis monkeys, crocodiles, and a variety of bird species.

  • Bornean Pygmy Elephants: Experience the rare Bornean Pygmy elephants in the wild, one of the world’s smallest elephant species. Sabah offers guided safaris to spot these unique elephants in their natural environment.

Cuisine & Delicacies

Sabah’s cuisine is a melting pot of flavors influenced by its ethnic diversity and natural abundance. The food culture is rich with fresh ingredients, seafood, and unique dishes.

  • Tuaran Mee: Tuaran Mee is a stir-fried noodle dish originating from Tuaran town, made with egg noodles, vegetables, and meat. It is an iconic local dish that’s flavorful and satisfying.

  • Sago: Sago is a traditional staple food in Sabah made from the starch of sago palm. It is often served as a side dish with fish or meat and is a unique part of the local cuisine.

  • Sabah Tea: Sabah’s very own organic tea, grown in the cool climate of the hills, is a refreshing beverage served hot or iced. It has a distinctive taste and is perfect for relaxing after a day of adventures.
