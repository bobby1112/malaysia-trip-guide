Sabah

Sabah, fondly known as the “Land Below the Wind”, is a captivating state located in the northern region of Borneo, Malaysia’s largest island. Framed by the sparkling waters of the South China Sea and the Sulu Sea, Sabah is a land of stunning contrasts — where majestic mountains, ancient rainforests, and vibrant coral reefs coexist in harmony.

At the heart of its natural wonders stands the iconic Mount Kinabalu, Southeast Asia’s highest peak and a UNESCO World Heritage Site that draws climbers and nature lovers from around the globe. Offshore, islands like Sipadan, Mabul, and Mataking boast some of the world’s most incredible diving spots, teeming with marine life and vibrant corals.

Sabah is not just a haven for outdoor adventurers — it is also a cultural mosaic, home to over 30 indigenous groups including the Kadazan-Dusun, Bajau, Murut, and Rungus. Each community brings unique traditions, languages, and festivals such as the Kaamatan Harvest Festival, where music, dance, and ancestral rituals come alive.

From the Sepilok Orangutan Rehabilitation Centre to the serene Kinabatangan River,Sabah’s rich biodiversity offers countless opportunities to connect with wildlife, including pygmy elephants, sun bears, and proboscis monkeys.The bustling capital city of Kota Kinabalu blends modernity with heritage, offering lively markets, delicious Sabahan cuisine, and warm hospitality.

Whether you seek adventure, tranquility, cultural immersion, or natural beauty, Sabah welcomes you with open arms and endless discoveries.
